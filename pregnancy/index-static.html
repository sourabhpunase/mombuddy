First Trimester: Weeks 1-12

The first trimester spans from week 1 through week 12 of pregnancy and is a critical period of development. During these initial weeks, your baby's major organs and body systems begin to form, including the brain, heart, and spinal cord.

By week 8, your baby is called a fetus and is about the size of a kidney bean. By the end of the first trimester, your baby will be about 3 inches long and weigh around 1 ounce.

Common symptoms you might experience include:
• Morning sickness (nausea and vomiting)
• Extreme fatigue
• Frequent urination
• Tender, swollen breasts
• Food aversions or cravings
• Mood swings
• Heartburn and constipation
